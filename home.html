About Mason

Mason Ritchason (b. 2000) is an American composer and musician with a highly diverse,

“melting-pot” musical background. Originally from Columbus, OH and raised in rural Mechanicsburg, OH,

Mason’s upbringing was pivotal in his compositional style. Early exposure to Alternative and Rock music

from the 1980s and 90s set him on an unorthodox path to classical composition that weaved through studies

in Jazz, Heavy Metal, and Punk genres.


The product of this unusual musical fostering is an interest in the intensities of life. Following in the

footsteps of mystic composers Olivier Messiaen and Alexander Scriabin, Mason expends his efforts delving

into the depths of emotions and the shared human experience. The intensity of joy, ecstasy, and hysteria

are dominant in the sounds he strives to produce, as well as mystery, uncertainty, and fear of the unknown.

Stylistic integrities in Mason’s music come from a place of fusion, combining academic and sophisticated

elements of classical music with the raw, powerful emotion of Hardcore, Heavy Metal, and Punk genres.

The extensive color intrinsic to Jazz influences Mason’s harmonic language,

where most of his musical experimentation takes place.


Mason is currently a student at Wittenberg University, from which he will receive two degrees in Music and

Computer Science in the Spring of 2024. Mason also spent three years studying Education, which he now researches

personally. As a trumpeter, he has performed extensively. During his time at Wittenberg, Mason has played in the

school’s Symphonic Band and Jazz Ensemble and has performed in several solo and chamber recitals. After graduation,

Mason plans to attend a prestigious graduate program where he will study Musical Composition and Theory,

constructing a strong portfolio for a career in Education at the Collegiate level.
